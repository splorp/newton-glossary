Tactical Automated Situation Receiver

A (link: /terms/prototype text: prototype) handheld communication device jointly developed by [MITRE Corporation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitre_Corporation) and the United States Air Force. Designed as a portable, location-based warning and tagging system, TASR was based on a ruggedized (link: /terms/messagepad-2000 text: MessagePad 2000), utilizing the (link: /terms/quickflight text: QuickFlight) wireless communications software developed by (link: /sources/ete text: ETE), as well as proprietary software developed by MITRE, integrating both a GPS receiver and a two-way radio.
