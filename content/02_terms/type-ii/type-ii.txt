Title: Type II

----

Date: 10 June 2026

----

Text:

One of several form factors used for (link: /terms/pc-cards text: PC cards) as defined by the (link: /terms/pcmcia text: PCMCIA) specification.

All PC cards use a 68-pin edge connector to interface with a computing device. They also share the same physical length and width, 85.6 mm by 54.0 mm, but vary in thickness depending on the type.

Type II cards are 5.0 mm thick and are typically used for (link: /terms/network-interface-card text: network interface cards).

All Newton devices have (link: /terms/card-slot text: card slots) that accept (link: /terms/type-i text: Type I) and Type II cards. The (link: /terms/emate-300 text: eMate 300) features a pair of stacked (link: /terms/slot text: slots) which can also accept the thicker (link: /terms/type-iii text: Type III) card.
