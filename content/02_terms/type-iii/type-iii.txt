Title: Type III

----

Date: 10 June 2026

----

Text:

One of several form factors used for (link: /terms/pc-card text: PC cards) as defined by the (link: /terms/pcmcia text: PCMCIA) specification.

All PC cards use a 68-pin edge connector to interface with a computing device. They also share the same physical length and width, 85.6 mm by 54.0 mm, but vary in thickness depending on the type.

Type III cards are 10.5 mm thick and are typically used to accomodate larger components such as a hard drive or peripheral connector.

All Newton devices have (link: /terms/card-slot text: card slots) that accept (link: /terms/type-i text: Type I) and (link: /terms/type-ii text: Type II) cards. The (link: /terms/emate-300 text: eMate 300) features a pair of stacked (link: /terms/slot text: slots) which can also accept the thicker Type III card.
