Title: Arrow Keys

----

Date: 29 March 2026

----

Text:

A set of four (link: /terms/function-key text: function keys) located on a keyboard that move the (link: /terms/caret text: caret) or text (link: /terms/insertion-point text: insertion point). These include up, down, left, and right arrow keys.

The left and right arrow keys move the caret left or right one (link: /terms/character text: character) at a time. The up and down arrow keys move the caret up or down one line of text at a time, aligning the caret at the nearest character boundary and keeping the caret as close as possible to the original horizontal position.

If text has been (link: /terms/select text: selected) and an arrow key is pressed, the text is (link: /terms/deselect text: deselected) and the caret appears at the right or left edge of the selection. The caret appears at the left edge of a text (link: /terms/selection text: selection) when the left arrow or up arrow key is pressed. The caret appears at the right edge of a text selection when the right arrow or down arrow key is pressed.

Also referred to as (link: /terms/cursor-keys text: Cursor Keys).

(link: /terms/scroll-arrows text: Scroll arrows) are sometimes referred to as arrow keys, even though they are (link: /terms/button text: buttons) on the (link: /terms/screen text: screen) and not keys on a keyboard.
